# Mortgage in Serbia — Guide

## General Conditions

Mortgage loans in Serbia are issued for up to **30 years**. The minimum down payment is usually **20%** of the property value, although some banks may require up to 30%.

Interest rates are typically linked to the **6-month EURIBOR** + the bank's fixed margin (usually 2.5–4%).

Banks typically use an annuity repayment scheme. Early repayment reduces the **payment amount**, not the loan term. It is processed through a visit to the bank branch.

The maximum monthly payment must not exceed **50%** of the borrower's official salary — this is the main approval criterion. A mortgage is only approved for a specific property — the bank will not consider an application without a selected apartment.

**Cash payments:** maximum **€10,000** in cash, anything above — only from a bank account.

**Area compliance:** when processing a mortgage, the bank sends an independent appraiser who takes measurements and photos of the property. The actual area must match the area registered in the cadastre. The maximum allowed discrepancy is **10%**.

## Specifics for foreigners

The mortgage approval process for foreigners takes **2+ months**. Before viewing a property, it's worth checking with the seller whether they are willing to wait for a credit buyer.

Sellers often require a **non-refundable deposit** — if the loan is not approved, the seller keeps a compensation amount for the waiting period (e.g. equivalent to rental income for that time). Deposit terms vary by seller.

## Banks Working with Foreigners

- ~~**Raiffeisen Bank** — one of the most foreigner-friendly~~
- **AIK Banka** — works with Russian-speaking clients. Early repayment of any amount without penalty — pays down the principal, reduces the monthly payment. Procedure: bring the amount to the branch, the manager processes a payment order and generates a new payment schedule. **Mandatory requirement: salary transfer to AIK**
- **API Banka** — works with Russian-speaking clients, but worse conditions: variable rate from the start. Early repayment up to 1,200,000 RSD/year without penalty, above that — 1% of the repayment amount. Via bank branch visit, reduces the payment amount

## Required Documents

1. Passport + copies
2. Residence permit (boravišna dozvola) or work visa
3. Income certificate / employment contract
4. Bank statement for the last 6 months
5. Preliminary purchase agreement
6. Property appraisal (arranged by the bank)

### Detailed list for the bank (for Russian citizens)

- Salary statement for the last 6 months (or from the start of employment if less)
- Bank account transaction history for the salary account over the last 6 months
- First white carton received upon entry to Serbia (if preserved)
- Employment contract with the current (Serbian) employer
- Employment contract with the previous employer (in Russia)
- Bank statement confirming availability of own funds (if any)
- Credit bureau report from Russia

## Property Transfer Tax

**Porez na prenos apsolutnih prava** — 2.5% of the assessed property value. Paid by the buyer within 15 days after registration.
